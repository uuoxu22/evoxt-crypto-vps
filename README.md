## Why Pay for a VPS with Cryptocurrency in the First Place?

There are a few genuinely useful reasons, and they're not all about being mysterious or avoiding taxes (though let's be honest, that's on some people's minds too).

**No banking restrictions.** If you're in a country where international wire transfers are painful, or where certain card networks just don't work reliably, crypto is a clean bypass. You don't need a Visa. You don't need a bank that supports USD transactions. You just need a wallet and a network connection.

**Privacy from payment processors.** When you pay with a credit card, that transaction goes through your bank, the payment processor, and often ends up in some marketing database somewhere. Crypto payments — especially on-chain Bitcoin or USDT — skip that whole pipeline. The hosting company still knows your email, but they don't have your card number or billing address.

**No currency conversion headaches.** If your savings are already sitting in BTC or ETH, paying in crypto means you skip the "convert to fiat, pay conversion fee, wait for settlement" dance entirely.

**Access for the unbanked.** According to Chainalysis data, a meaningful chunk of the global population still lacks reliable access to traditional banking. Crypto VPS hosting is, weirdly enough, a real accessibility story as much as a privacy one.

That said — a quick reality check. Bitcoin transactions are publicly visible on the blockchain. If someone is determined to trace a payment, they can. True anonymity requires something like Monero. Most mainstream VPS providers that accept crypto (including Evoxt) support Bitcoin and/or stablecoins like USDT, which offer privacy but not full anonymity. Know the difference before you assume you're invisible.
